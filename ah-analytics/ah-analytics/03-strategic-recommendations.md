# Strategic Recommendations

Derived from 120 long-form videos and 42 shorts. Ordered by expected impact.

---

## 1. Resolve the pillar/demand mismatch

**The problem:** AH Brain's largest research pillar (circadian & light, 11 docs) maps to
the channel's weakest topic (0.52× long-form, 0.41× shorts). Its strongest topic
(carnivore/animal-based, 1.25×, 7 of 12 outliers) is well covered but not the archive's
centre of gravity.

**Three honest options:**

**A. Follow the data.** Lead with carnivore/animal-based, treat circadian as supporting
material. Lowest risk, but abandons the direction the archive was clearly built toward.

**B. Bridge it.** Keep circadian as the destination, but enter through the proven door.
This is the recommended path — see section 2.

**C. Commit to the pivot anyway.** Accept a metrics dip to build a different channel.
Legitimate if circadian is where the brand's conviction is, but do it knowingly, not
by accident.

The one thing to avoid is the current default: **investing the most research effort in
the least-proven topic without a bridging strategy.**

---

## 2. How to bridge circadian into a carnivore audience

The evidence says this audience wants **personal experience + practical outcome +
honest difficulty**. Circadian content has been published as *abstract explainers* —
the exact format that fails on this channel.

Compare the two "9 Ways" videos:

- "9 Ways To Connect With Your Ancestors Through Food" → **1,100 views**
- "9 WAYS TO ADD MORE FAT ON AN ANIMAL BASED DIET" → **15,000 views**

Same structure. **13× difference.** The variable is abstract vs actionable.

### Reframe the circadian material

| Current framing (underperformed) | Bridged framing |
|---|---|
| "Circadian Rhythm" | "I Watched The Sunrise Every Day For 30 Days" |
| "Grounding Health Benefits" | "The Free Thing That Fixed My Sleep On Carnivore" |
| "Shift Work & Circadian Rhythm" | "Why Carnivore Stopped Working — It Wasn't The Food" |
| "Master Your Circadian" | "5 Things No One Tells You About Light And Energy" |

That last one deliberately mirrors the #1 video's title structure.

**The strongest bridge available:** `ancestral-tips-to-wake-up-earlier-short.md` in the
archive already connects nutrition → circadian. That's the natural crossover asset.

## 3. Make more long videos

**The 20+ minute bucket returns 1.70× — the best of any duration — and there are only
8 of them in 120 videos.**

The #1 video is 28 minutes. The #2 is 23. Meanwhile 11 videos under 5 minutes average
0.33×.

The archive is well-suited to this: the **"Rise of Seed Oils" 4-chapter documentary
script** is the most obvious unexploited long-form asset in AH Brain. Documentary
framing + 20+ minutes + a topic adjacent to proven demand (seed oils, food industry)
is the single highest-expected-value production in the library.

---

## 4. Fix the titles

Three cheap, immediate changes:

1. **Stop defaulting to ALL CAPS.** 31 videos use it; it returns 0.56×. It reads as
   volume, not value.
2. **Stop asking questions.** 8 attempts, 0.42×, zero outliers. Convert every question
   title into a claim: "Are Plant-Based Milks Healthy?" → "What's Actually In Plant-Based Milk."
3. **Use how-to framing more.** Highest ratio (1.03×) and used only 7 times.

---

## 5. Lead with honest difficulty

The two strongest videos are both about problems, not benefits:

- "CARNIVORE DIET: 5 Things That No One Talks About" — 71,000
- "5 REASONS WHY CARNIVORE MAY NOT BE WORKING" — 29,000

This is a real editorial finding and it aligns well with the fact-checking discipline in
`EDITORIAL-GUIDELINES.md`. **Content that admits limitations outperforms content that
only advocates.** The raw-milk doc — which openly flags that the evidence is limited and
conflicting — is the archive's best model for this voice.

Concrete formats: "What I Got Wrong About…", "Why I Stopped…", "5 Things No One Tells
You About…". The archive already contains "Why I No Longer Do Extended Fasting"
(13,000, 1.77×) as proof.

## 6. Publishing cadence

TubeLab's most recent publish estimate for this channel is **mid-2023**. Its date fields
are coarse (everything buckets to single days), so treat this as a flag to verify rather
than a fact — but if the channel has genuinely been dormant, that's the largest single
factor in current performance, and no content strategy compensates for it.

**Verify in YouTube Studio before acting on anything else here.**

## What this analysis can't tell you

TubeLab sees public metrics only. **Retention, click-through rate, traffic source and
subscriber conversion are missing** — and those determine *why* a video worked, not just
that it did. A 71,000-view video with 20% retention means something very different from
one with 60%.

Export the YouTube Studio data (Analytics → Advanced Mode → Export) and this analysis
becomes considerably more actionable.
