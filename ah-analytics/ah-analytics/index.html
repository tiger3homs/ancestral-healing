The headline finding

Where the archive points vs where the audience is
⚠ Strategic mismatch

The biggest research pillar maps to the worst-performing topic.

Carnivore and animal-based content returns 1.25× the channel average and holds 7 of the 12 outliers — despite being only a third of the catalogue.

Circadian and light returns 0.52× in long-form and 0.41× in shorts — the weakest category on the channel. Yet it is the largest pillar in AH Brain, with 11 of 29 documents.

This doesn't mean the circadian research is wasted. It means it has been published as abstract explainers, and that format fails here regardless of topic. Section 04 shows the fix.

What this analysis cannot see

TubeLab reads public metrics only. Missing: retention, click-through rate, traffic source, and subscriber conversion — the numbers that explain why something worked.

  • View counts are rounded estimates — directionally reliable, not exact
  • Publish dates are coarse and bucket to single days; don't use them for trends
  • Sample covers 120 of 208 videos (58%)
  • Circadian long-form has only 2 data points — that verdict is provisional
  • Competitive channel data unavailable (TubeLab credit balance is 0)
