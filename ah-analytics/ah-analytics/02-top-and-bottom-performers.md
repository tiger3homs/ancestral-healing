### The winning formula

Reading across all 12, the pattern is consistent:

1. **Carnivore or animal-based framing** — 7 of 12 explicitly.
2. **Long** — the top two are 28 and 23 minutes. Median outlier length ~13 min.
3. **Personal experience or transformation** — "I Eat Burgers with EVERY Meal,"
   "I Quit Sugar For 3 Years," "First Month … WHAT TO EXPECT." Lived experience beats
   abstract education.
4. **Addresses a problem or objection** — "5 Things No One Talks About," "5 Reasons Why
   Carnivore May NOT Be Working." Content that names the *downside* of the diet
   outperforms content that only sells it.
5. **Practical and specific** — recipes, food lists, "9 ways to add more fat."

**The single strongest sub-pattern: honest content about the difficulties.** The #1 and
#3 videos are both about what goes *wrong*. That's a trust signal the audience rewards.

| 1,500 | Are Plant-Based Milks Healthy? | Question hook; answer implied and unsurprising |
| 1,600 | A Guide to Ancestral Healing — NEW EBOOK! | Pure promo |
| 1,700 | A RECIPE FOR DISASTER — Part 2 | Sequels underperform originals (Part 1: 7,300) |

### The failure patterns

- **Topic-as-title.** "Circadian Rhythm," "OMAD," "The C Word" — these name a subject
  without promising anything. Compare to "Avoiding the Sun Linked to Health Problems,"
  the best-performing circadian short, which names a *consequence*.
- **Channel-inward content.** Announcements, achievements, ebook promos all land near
  the floor. The audience shows up for their own problems, not the channel's news.
- **Question titles.** Zero outliers across 8 attempts.
- **Sequels.** "A Recipe for Disaster" got 7,300; Part 2 got 1,700 — a 77% drop.
- **Abstract over practical.** "9 Ways To Connect With Your Ancestors Through Food"
  (1,100) vs "9 WAYS TO ADD MORE FAT ON AN ANIMAL BASED DIET" (15,000). Same
  construction, same number — one is philosophical, one is actionable. **13× difference.**

That last comparison is the cleanest lesson in the dataset.
