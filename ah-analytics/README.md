## ⚠️ The headline finding

**The channel's proven audience and the AH Brain content library are pointed in
different directions.**

| | Published performance | AH Brain coverage |
|---|---|---|
| **Carnivore / animal-based** | **1.25× median ratio** · 7 of 12 outliers | 13 docs |
| **Circadian & light** | **0.52× long-form, 0.41× shorts (worst category)** | **11 docs — the largest pillar** |

Videos with "carnivore" in the title: **9,200 median views**.
Everything else: **3,700 median views**.

That is a **2.5× gap**, on a sample of 120 videos.

Meanwhile the archive's single biggest research investment — the circadian and light
pillar — maps to the **worst-performing** topic on the channel. The seven circadian
shorts average 1,900 median views against a 3,100 shorts median. Grounding (1,200),
Circadian Rhythm (1,300) and Shift Work (1,400) are among the weakest posts on the
entire channel.

**This does not mean the circadian material is worthless.** It means it has not yet
been packaged in a way this audience responds to. See `03-strategic-recommendations.md`
for how to bridge it.

## What actually works

**Topic** — carnivore leads by a wide margin (1.25× vs 0.34–0.69× for everything else).

**Duration** — longer is better, decisively:

| Length | n | Median views | Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| <5 min | 11 | 2,400 | 0.33× |
| 5–8 min | 39 | 5,900 | 0.80× |
| 8–12 min | 41 | 4,600 | 0.62× |
| 12–20 min | 21 | 6,400 | 0.87× |
| **20+ min** | **8** | **12,500** | **1.70×** |

The top video is 28 minutes. The second is 22. Short videos underperform badly.

**Hook style** — how-to (1.03×) and contrarian (0.77×) beat questions (0.42×) and
listicles (0.50×). ALL-CAPS titles underperform (0.56×) despite being used 31 times.

## Data caveats

- **Views are TubeLab estimates**, rounded (e.g. "71,000"). Directionally reliable,
  not exact. Cross-check against YouTube Studio.
- **Publish dates are coarse estimates** and bucket to single days — do not use them
  for trend-over-time analysis.
- **Sample is 120 of 208 videos** (58%). Skewed toward more recent uploads.
- **No retention, CTR, or traffic-source data.** TubeLab sees public metrics only.
  Those live in YouTube Studio and would sharpen everything here considerably.
- Related-channel competitive data was unavailable (TubeLab credit balance is 0).
