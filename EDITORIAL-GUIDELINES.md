Brand voice, claim handling, and fact-checking rules for the AH Brain.

## Brand voice

From `circadian-content-ideas.md`, the archive's own voice definition:

> Bold, provocative, confident, disruptively educational — rebel scientist meets
> renegade neurosurgeon, bringing physics and quantum thinking into biology.

**In practice:**

- **Hooks are contrarian and specific.** "They lied to you about cataracts." "Your
  retina is a quantum computer." Not "Here are 5 tips for better sleep."
- **Lead with the mechanism, not the instruction.** The audience is here because the
  *why* feels hidden from them. Explain the system, then give the action.
- **Simple actions, big framing.** The core advice is free and easy — watch the
  sunrise, go barefoot, eat the organ meat. The framing is what makes it feel urgent.
- **Reconnection, not restriction.** The story is returning to something ancestral,
  not depriving yourself. "We're not reinventing the wheel — we're returning to
  traditional ways of living and eating."
- **Gratitude and community.** Support local farmers, know your food source, share
  the meal. This is a recurring warm counterweight to the critical material.

**Avoid:** generic wellness filler, hedging into meaninglessness, and — importantly —
stating contested claims in the flat voice of settled fact.

## ⚠️ Claim handling — read before generating anything

This archive contains three tiers of claim. **Treat them differently.**

### Tier 1 — Well-supported, state plainly

- Circadian rhythm meaningfully affects sleep, metabolism and hormone regulation
- Morning daylight exposure helps anchor the sleep-wake cycle
- Ultra-processed food consumption is associated with poor health outcomes
- Excessive evening screen light can suppress melatonin and disrupt sleep
- Grass-fed and grain-fed beef have measurably different fatty-acid profiles
- Regenerative farming practices improve soil health and biodiversity
- Gum disease is associated with systemic inflammation and cardiovascular risk
- Microplastics and PFAS are detectable in human tissue and are an active concern

### Tier 2 — Plausible but oversold, add qualifiers

- Red / near-infrared light therapy benefits (real research exists, but effect sizes
  in the archive's guide are stated far more confidently than the literature supports)
- Raw milk's enzyme and probiotic advantages (the archive's own doc flags that
  evidence is limited and conflicting — **keep that flag**)
- Organ meats as uniquely nutrient-dense (true they're nutrient-dense; "transform
  your health" is marketing)
- Grounding / earthing (early-stage evidence, frequently overstated)
- Anti-nutrients (real compounds with real effects; the practical significance for
  a varied diet is heavily disputed)

### Tier 3 — Contested or fringe, attribute or omit

**Never state these in the brand's own voice as fact.** Attribute them to the person
who said them, or leave them out:

- Sun exposure preventing breast or prostate cancer
- 5G / EMF causing cancer, Alzheimer's or Parkinson's spikes
- Deuterium ("dyum") theory of metabolism
- "Cancer is a mitochondrial disease, not genetic"
- DARPA / big-tech blue-light behavioural-control narratives
- Vitamin D supplements being harmful
- Claims that sunscreen or sunglasses cause disease
- Any suggestion of avoiding or replacing conventional medical care

## Fact-check protocol

Before any script, reel, carousel or guide ships:

1. **Tier the claims.** Anything Tier 3 gets attributed, softened, or cut.
2. **Verify every statistic and study citation.** Several study references in the
   archive are loosely cited (journal + year, no title or authors). Find the actual
   paper or drop the number.
3. **Attribute contested claims by name.** "Dr. Jack Kruse argues that…" — not
   "Science shows that…"
4. **Keep the disclaimer.** Educational content, not medical advice. Encourage
   consulting a qualified professional, especially for anything touching a
   diagnosis, medication, pregnancy, or a child's health.
5. **Never discourage medical care.** The archive contains anti-establishment
   framing about doctors and the medical system. Critique of incentives is fair
   game; telling people not to seek treatment is not.

### Extra caution required

Content touching these areas needs a higher bar and explicit disclaimers:

- **Pregnancy and infants** — the archive has claims about blue light and fetal
  development, and raw dairy
- **Cancer** — multiple Tier 3 claims
- **Children** — screen time, homeschooling, sun exposure, vaccination framing
- **Post-surgical recovery** — the appendicitis protocol is not a substitute for
  post-op medical guidance
- **Fasting and restriction** — including the "dry fasting" mention; avoid
  prescribing specific fasting protocols
