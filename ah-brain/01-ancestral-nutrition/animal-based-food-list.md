# Animal-Based Food List

> **Asset type:** One-page infographic (image-only PDF, transcribed via OCR).
> A "what to eat / what to avoid" reference chart for the animal-based diet.

## ✅ What to eat — core proteins

| Food | Note |
|---|---|
| Beef | Grass-fed and finished |
| Pork | Pasture-raised |
| Chicken | Pasture-raised |
| Eggs | Corn/soy-free |
| Fish | Wild-caught |
| Organ meat | Liver, heart, kidney |
| Bone broth | Collagen source |

## ✅ Low-toxicity additions

- Grass-fed butter / ghee
- Tallow / suet
- Raw, local honey
- Raw dairy

**Sweet fruit** (avoid the seeds): banana, berries, mango, pineapple, watermelon, papaya, oranges

**Non-sweet fruit** (seedless / skinless): avocado, pumpkin, squash, zucchini, cucumber

**Roots & tubers:** sweet potato

## ⛔ Considered toxic / to avoid

- Grains and gluten — bread, pasta, noodles, cakes, crackers, pies, pastries, tortilla chips
- Legumes and peanuts
- Rice
- Celery
- Mushrooms
- **Alliums** — onion, garlic, leek
- **Nightshades** — tomato, white potato, eggplant, peppers, chili peppers
- Goji berries
- **High-heavy-metal fish** — tuna, king mackerel, halibut, sea bass
- Processed meats
- Gravies, sauces and salad dressings

---

> **Note:** This list reflects the strict animal-based framework used across this
> archive. Several exclusions (alliums, mushrooms, nightshades) go beyond mainstream
> dietary guidance and are editorial positions, not established consensus.
