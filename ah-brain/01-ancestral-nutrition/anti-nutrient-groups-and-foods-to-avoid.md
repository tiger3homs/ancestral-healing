# ANTI NUTRIENT GROUPS AND FOODS TO AVOID

**ANTI NUTRIENT GROUPS AND FOODS TO AVOID**

1. **Phytates**: Phytates are naturally occurring compounds found in many plant-based foods. They bind to minerals like iron, zinc, and calcium in the digestive system, forming complexes that are difficult for the body to absorb. This can hinder the absorption of these essential minerals, potentially leading to deficiencies if the diet relies heavily on phytate-rich foods.

2. **Oxalates**: Oxalates are compounds found in certain plant foods. When consumed in high amounts, oxalates can bind with calcium to form crystals, leading to the formation of kidney stones in susceptible individuals. Additionally, oxalates can interfere with the absorption of calcium and other minerals in the gut, potentially affecting overall mineral balance.

3. **Lectins**: Lectins are proteins found in many plant foods, especially legumes and grains. Some lectins can resist digestion and bind to the lining of the gut, potentially causing irritation and inflammation. They can also interfere with the absorption of nutrients by disrupting the integrity of the intestinal lining.

4. **Protease inhibitors**: Protease inhibitors are compounds found in certain foods, particularly soybeans and other legumes. As the name suggests, they inhibit the activity of enzymes called proteases, which are responsible for breaking down proteins during digestion. This interference with protein digestion can reduce the availability of amino acids, which are the building blocks of proteins.

5. **Tannins**: Tannins are naturally occurring compounds found in various plant foods and beverages. They can bind to proteins and other nutrients in the digestive system, making them less accessible for absorption. Tannins can also interfere with digestive enzymes, potentially affecting the breakdown and absorption of nutrients.

6. **Enzyme inhibitors**: Enzyme inhibitors are substances that hinder the activity of specific enzymes in the body. For example, certain enzyme inhibitors found in soybeans and other legumes can interfere with the digestion of proteins, carbohydrates, and fats, potentially affecting the overall nutrient absorption and utilization.

**1. Phytates:**

- Whole grains: wheat, barley, rye, oats, rice, corn, millet

- Legumes: beans (kidney beans, black beans, pinto beans, navy beans), lentils, chickpeas, soybeans

- Nuts and seeds: almonds, walnuts, pecans, cashews, sesame seeds, flaxseeds

**2. Oxalates:**

- Leafy greens: spinach, kale, Swiss chard, beet greens, collard greens

- Vegetables: rhubarb, beets, okra, leeks, celery, eggplant

- Fruits: figs, blackberries, raspberries, kiwifruit, concord grapes, plums

- Nuts and seeds: almonds, cashews, peanuts, sesame seeds, poppy seeds

- Grains: wheat bran, quinoa, amaranth, buckwheat

**3. Lectins:**

- Legumes: kidney beans, soybeans, lentils, chickpeas, lima beans, black beans

- Nightshade vegetables: tomatoes, potatoes, eggplants, peppers

- Grains: wheat, barley, rye, corn, oats

**4. Protease inhibitors:**

- Soybeans and soy products: tofu, tempeh, soy milk, edamame

- Legumes: kidney beans, lima beans, navy beans, black-eyed peas

**5. Tannins:**

- Tea: black tea, green tea

- Coffee

- Red wine

- Pomegranates

- Berries: raspberries, strawberries, blueberries, cranberries, blackberries

**6. Enzyme inhibitors:**

- Soybeans and soy products: tofu, soy milk, soy protein isolate

- Legumes: kidney beans, lentils, lima beans, chickpeas
