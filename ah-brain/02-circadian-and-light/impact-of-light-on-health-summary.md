# Impact of Light on Health — bioSpectral (summary)

> **Third-party document.** Published by BioSpectral Systems ("Visible Light Hygiene").
> The original PDF is kept in `/source-pdfs/`. Summarized here rather than reproduced
> in full, since it is not our own material.

## Core thesis

Light — both visible and non-visible — is presented as the single most influential
environmental factor in human health, ahead of diet, exercise and toxins. The
argument is that addressing your light environment is the *foundational* step, with
everything else built on top of it.

## Key arguments

- **Indoor living is the underappreciated shift.** The paper claims the average
  person in the USA spent roughly 22 hours a day indoors as of 2023, against a
  species history of living outdoors continuously.
- **The body as a photoelectric system.** Humans are framed as a hydrated, carbon
  photoelectric system that stores captured solar charge in the body's water network
  and in melanin as DC electricity, running on a net negative charge that requires
  constant electron input.
- **Disease as energy deficit.** Most disease is attributed to a lack of energy in
  the body rather than to nuclear DNA mutation — a biophysical rather than genetic
  model of illness.
- **Light activates the whole system.** Light entering via eyes and skin is said to
  trigger the hypothalamus, which governs the autonomic nervous system, endocrine
  system, pituitary and the biological clock.
- **"Animal photosynthesis."** Animals are described as having their own adaptation
  of the plant kingdom's reliance on the 24-hour light/dark cycle.
- **We engineered red and infrared out of our buildings.** Fire, kerosene and
  incandescent bulbs preserved much of the sun's red/infrared share indoors. Banning
  incandescents, adding double-glazed tinted windows, and using modern insulated
  concrete/brick/gyprock construction stripped that spectrum out and removed the
  seasonal variation our biology expects.
- **Dirty electricity and AC fields.** The alternating-current grid, wireless
  frequencies and magnetic fields are described as compounding the artificial-light
  problem into an "alien ecosystem" for human biology.
- **Food carries a light signature.** Eating in-season, local food is framed as
  matching the light signature of your environment; out-of-season food sends
  conflicting energy/information signals.
