## Summary of "Light: Medicine of the Future" by Jacob Liberman

**Overview**

"Light: Medicine of the Future" by Dr. Jacob Liberman explores the therapeutic potential of light and color, challenging the widespread belief that sunlight is inherently dangerous. The book argues that modern lifestyles and technologies—such as fluorescent lighting, sunglasses, tanning lotions, and excessive time spent indoors—may be more detrimental to health than sunlight itself[4](https://www.innertraditions.com/books/light-medicine-of-the-future)[5](https://www.target.com/p/light-medicine-of-the-future-by-jacob-liberman-paperback/-/A-91162569)[6](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/892575.Light).

**Core Themes**

- **Healing with Light:** Liberman presents evidence and case studies showing the use of light therapy in treating a wide range of conditions, including cancer, depression, stress, visual problems, PMS, sexual dysfunction, learning disabilities, and immune system disorders[1](https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/Light-Medicine-of-the-Future/Jacob-Liberman/9781879181014)[4](https://www.innertraditions.com/books/light-medicine-of-the-future)[5](https://www.target.com/p/light-medicine-of-the-future-by-jacob-liberman-paperback/-/A-91162569)[7](https://books.google.com/books/about/Light_Medicine_of_the_Future.html?id=SIqowKsqn3UC)[8](https://books.apple.com/us/book/light-medicine-of-the-future/id1249515204).

- **Full-Spectrum Light vs. Artificial Light:** The book emphasizes the health benefits of exposure to full-spectrum natural light, contrasting it with the negative effects of artificial lighting. Liberman draws on research, such as John Ott’s work, to illustrate how full-spectrum light can improve cholesterol levels, learning outcomes, and overall well-being[4](https://www.innertraditions.com/books/light-medicine-of-the-future)[6](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/892575.Light).

- **Holistic Health Approach:** Liberman advocates for a holistic model of health that integrates mind and body, envisioning a future where medical interventions use light and color instead of drugs and invasive procedures. He describes a scenario where lasers replace scalpels, phototherapy replaces chemotherapy, and prescription colors replace pharmaceutical drugs[2](https://meridian.allenpress.com/ijyt/article-pdf/3/1/49/1737769/ijyt_3_1_13mm2785718lg444.pdf).

- **Educational and Environmental Applications:** The book discusses how proper lighting and color schemes in classrooms can enhance learning and reduce behavioral problems, suggesting that environmental design plays a crucial role in health and development[6](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/892575.Light).

- **Scientific and Historical Context:** Liberman references both modern scientific studies and historical practices, such as the use of light in ancient Egyptian healing temples, to support his claims[6](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/892575.Light).

- **Myth-Busting:** The book challenges the notion that UV light and sunlight are primarily harmful, instead highlighting their essential role in processes like vitamin D synthesis and mood regulation. Liberman critiques the fear-driven avoidance of sunlight, suggesting it may contribute to health problems[4](https://www.innertraditions.com/books/light-medicine-of-the-future)[5](https://www.target.com/p/light-medicine-of-the-future-by-jacob-liberman-paperback/-/A-91162569).

**Structure and Features**

- The book is divided into three main parts:

  - **Part 1:** Reviews the science of light and its physiological effects.

  - **Part 2:** Details clinical applications of light therapy, including case studies and pioneering research.

  - **Part 3:** Presents a vision for the future of medicine, emphasizing holistic, light-based therapies and the interconnectedness of all aspects of health[2](https://meridian.allenpress.com/ijyt/article-pdf/3/1/49/1737769/ijyt_3_1_13mm2785718lg444.pdf)[4](https://www.innertraditions.com/books/light-medicine-of-the-future).

- Includes practical resources, such as lists of full-spectrum light products, practitioners, and further reading[2](https://meridian.allenpress.com/ijyt/article-pdf/3/1/49/1737769/ijyt_3_1_13mm2785718lg444.pdf).

**Key Takeaways**

- Light is a fundamental, underutilized tool for healing and maintaining health.

- Many common ailments may be alleviated or prevented through appropriate exposure to natural light and color.

- Modern technological advancements in lighting and lifestyle may inadvertently contribute to "malillumination," a deficiency of beneficial light exposure[4](https://www.innertraditions.com/books/light-medicine-of-the-future).

- The future of medicine may see a shift toward non-invasive, light-based therapies that treat the whole person rather than just symptoms[2](https://meridian.allenpress.com/ijyt/article-pdf/3/1/49/1737769/ijyt_3_1_13mm2785718lg444.pdf).

**Conclusion**

"Light: Medicine of the Future" is both a scientific and visionary work, blending research, clinical experience, and holistic philosophy. Liberman’s writing is accessible and passionate, making a compelling case for the integration of light therapy into mainstream health and wellness practices[2](https://meridian.allenpress.com/ijyt/article-pdf/3/1/49/1737769/ijyt_3_1_13mm2785718lg444.pdf)[4](https://www.innertraditions.com/books/light-medicine-of-the-future)[5](https://www.target.com/p/light-medicine-of-the-future-by-jacob-liberman-paperback/-/A-91162569)[6](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/892575.Light).

## John Ott’s Work on Full Spectrum Light and Its Role in Well-Being

**John Ott’s Contributions**

John Ott, often called the "father of full-spectrum lighting," pioneered research into how exposure to the full spectrum of natural sunlight affects living organisms—including plants, animals, and humans[1](https://ottlite.com/pages/dr-ott-the-original)[3](https://www.makegreatlight.com/about-us/blog/dr-ott-pioneer-of-full-spectrum-light)[5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Ott). His work began with time-lapse photography for Walt Disney, where he discovered that the growth and even gender expression of plants could be manipulated by altering the quality and spectrum of artificial light[1](https://ottlite.com/pages/dr-ott-the-original)[3](https://www.makegreatlight.com/about-us/blog/dr-ott-pioneer-of-full-spectrum-light)[5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Ott). This led him to investigate the broader biological effects of light.

**Key Findings and Theories**

- **Full Spectrum Light Is Essential:** Ott’s experiments demonstrated that only full-spectrum light, which includes all wavelengths found in natural sunlight (including some infrared and ultraviolet), supports optimal health and development in plants, animals, and humans[2](https://scienceoflight.org/home/)[5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Ott). He coined the term "malillumination" to describe the health problems arising from insufficient exposure to natural light, likening it to malnutrition[3](https://www.makegreatlight.com/about-us/blog/dr-ott-pioneer-of-full-spectrum-light).

- **Impact on Human Health:** Ott found that the absence of properly balanced light could lead to various health disorders. He argued that modern lifestyles—characterized by time spent indoors, use of artificial lighting, and shielding from sunlight—contribute to this deficiency[3](https://www.makegreatlight.com/about-us/blog/dr-ott-pioneer-of-full-spectrum-light)[5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Ott).

- **Educational and Behavioral Benefits:** Ott’s classroom research showed that installing full-spectrum lighting improved students’ academic performance, behavior, resistance to fatigue, and overall attitudes within just one month[4](https://scienceoflight.org/scholastic-performance/). He concluded that many people are "light deficient," and this deficiency may underlie both physical and emotional problems[4](https://scienceoflight.org/scholastic-performance/).

- **Technological Innovations:** Ott developed one of the first full-spectrum fluorescent tubes, called Vitalite, and founded a lighting company to bring these products to the public[8](https://www.ottbiolight.com/drott.htm). His work inspired further research and the development of lighting products designed to mimic natural daylight for indoor environments[6](https://ottlite.com/pages/why-ottlite).

**Relation to Light Medicine**

Ott’s research is foundational to the field of "light medicine," which advocates for the use of natural or full-spectrum light as a therapeutic tool for overall well-being. His findings support the premise that exposure to the full range of sunlight’s wavelengths is not only beneficial but necessary for optimal health, influencing everything from mood and immune function to learning and behavior[3](https://www.makegreatlight.com/about-us/blog/dr-ott-pioneer-of-full-spectrum-light)[4](https://scienceoflight.org/scholastic-performance/)[5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Ott). The principles established by Ott are frequently cited in works like Jacob Liberman’s "Light: Medicine of the Future," which expands on the idea that light is a vital, underutilized element in holistic health care.

## Health Disorders Caused by Malillumination According to Dr. John Ott

Dr. John Ott identified "malillumination"—a lack of exposure to full-spectrum natural light—as a root cause of various health disorders. According to his research, the following conditions and symptoms are associated with malillumination:

- **Functional Disorders of the Nervous System:** Insufficient natural light can disrupt the nervous system, potentially affecting mood, cognition, and neurological health[1](https://www.makegreatlight.com/about-us/blog/dr-ott-pioneer-of-full-spectrum-light)[5](https://vitamindwiki.com/tiki-download_wiki_attachment.php?attId=28).

- **Vitamin D Deficiency:** Lack of sunlight impairs the body's ability to produce vitamin D, leading to deficiencies that impact bone health and immune function[1](https://www.makegreatlight.com/about-us/blog/dr-ott-pioneer-of-full-spectrum-light)[5](https://vitamindwiki.com/tiki-download_wiki_attachment.php?attId=28).

- **Weakening of the Body’s Defenses:** Reduced exposure to full-spectrum light weakens the immune system, increasing susceptibility to infections and chronic diseases[1](https://www.makegreatlight.com/about-us/blog/dr-ott-pioneer-of-full-spectrum-light)[5](https://vitamindwiki.com/tiki-download_wiki_attachment.php?attId=28).

- **Aggravation of Chronic Diseases:** Chronic health conditions may worsen in the absence of adequate natural light[1](https://www.makegreatlight.com/about-us/blog/dr-ott-pioneer-of-full-spectrum-light)[5](https://vitamindwiki.com/tiki-download_wiki_attachment.php?attId=28).

- **Hormonal Imbalances:** Disrupted circadian rhythms from poor lighting affect the regulation of hormones such as serotonin, melatonin, and insulin, leading to sleep disorders, mood disturbances, and metabolic issues[5](https://vitamindwiki.com/tiki-download_wiki_attachment.php?attId=28).

- **Fatigue and Reduced Vitality:** People often experience tiredness and a general lack of energy when deprived of full-spectrum light[5](https://vitamindwiki.com/tiki-download_wiki_attachment.php?attId=28)[7](https://scienceoflight.org/the-indoor-pandemic/).

- **Depression and Mood Disorders:** Malillumination is linked to depression, including seasonal affective disorder (SAD), and other mood-related problems[5](https://vitamindwiki.com/tiki-download_wiki_attachment.php?attId=28)[7](https://scienceoflight.org/the-indoor-pandemic/).

- **Weight Gain and Metabolic Issues:** Disrupted circadian rhythms and hormonal imbalances can contribute to weight gain and metabolic dysfunction[5](https://vitamindwiki.com/tiki-download_wiki_attachment.php?attId=28)[7](https://scienceoflight.org/the-indoor-pandemic/).

- **Sleep Disorders:** Poor light exposure can result in insomnia and other sleep problems due to melatonin disruption[5](https://vitamindwiki.com/tiki-download_wiki_attachment.php?attId=28).

- **Headaches and Pain:** Chronic lack of natural light may contribute to headaches and general pain[5](https://vitamindwiki.com/tiki-download_wiki_attachment.php?attId=28).

- **PMS and Reproductive Issues:** Malillumination has been associated with premenstrual syndrome and reduced fertility[5](https://vitamindwiki.com/tiki-download_wiki_attachment.php?attId=28).

- **Lowered Immune Response:** The immune system’s effectiveness may be compromised, increasing vulnerability to illness[5](https://vitamindwiki.com/tiki-download_wiki_attachment.php?attId=28).

- **Learning and Behavioral Problems:** In school studies, full-spectrum lighting improved behaviour, learning outcomes, and reduced dental cavities, suggesting that malillumination can negatively impact cognitive and behavioural development[6](https://www.westonaprice.org/the-effects-of-artificial-light-and-other-environmental-factors-on-human-health-and-behavior-by-dr-john-ott/).

- **Increased Tumor Development (in animal studies):** Ott’s animal research found that exposure to certain artificial light conditions led to a higher incidence of tumors, abnormal calcium deposits, and behavioral disturbances[2](https://www.motherearthnews.com/sustainable-living/nature-and-environment/john-ott-zm0z86zhun/).

Dr. Ott’s work underscores the importance of regular exposure to full-spectrum natural light for maintaining physical, emotional, and cognitive health[1](https://www.makegreatlight.com/about-us/blog/dr-ott-pioneer-of-full-spectrum-light)[2](https://www.motherearthnews.com/sustainable-living/nature-and-environment/john-ott-zm0z86zhun/)[5](https://vitamindwiki.com/tiki-download_wiki_attachment.php?attId=28).
