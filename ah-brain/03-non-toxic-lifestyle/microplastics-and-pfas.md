# Microplastics and PFAS

Study Title “Microplastics in Human Placenta”

Summary: The groundbreaking study discovered microplastics in the placentas of unborn babies, indicating that these particles can cross the placental barrier and potentially affect fetal development.

Study Title “Association of Perfluoroalkyl Substances with Thyroid Function in Pregnant Women and Neonates”

Summary: This study found a link between exposure to PFAS during pregnancy and altered thyroid function in both pregnant women and their newborns, raising concerns about the impact on fetal development.

Study Title “Plastic Ingestion by Humans: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis”

Summary: This meta-analysis examined numerous studies on plastic ingestion by humans and found that a significant portion of the global population ingests microplastics through food, water and air potentially leading to various health risks.

Study Title “Plastic Teabags Release Billions of Microparticles and Nanoparticles into Tea”

Summary: Researchers discovered that plastic tea bags release billions of micro and nanoparticles when steeped in hot water, suggesting that consuming tea from plastic tea bags may expose individuals to plastic particles.

Study Title “Endocrine-Disrupting Chemicals in Plastic Bottles: Are they Harmful to Human Health?”

Summary: This study reviewed the literature on endocrine-distrupting chemicals (EDCs) found in plastic bottles and highlighted their potential adverse effects on human health including hormonal imbalance and reproductive issues.

Study Title “Association Between Phthalates Exposure and Metabolic Syndrome in Adults: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

Summary: This systematic review and meta-analysis revealed a positive association between exposure to phthalates, commonly found in plastics and the risk of metabolic syndrome in adults, indicating a potential link between plastic chemicals and chronic diseases.

Study Title “Plastic Pollution and its Impact on Human Health”

Summary: This comprehensive review examined the various ways in which plastic pollution affects human health, including exposure to harmful chemicals leached from plastics, ingestion of microplastics and the potential for respiratory issues due to plastic combustion.

Study Title “Bisphenol A and it Alternatives: Are they Safe?”

Summary: This study evaluated the safety of bisphenol A (BPA) and its alternatives used in plastic products and concluded that many of these chemicals pose health risks, including reproductive toxicity, developmental abnormalities and metabolic disorders.

Study Title “Exposure to Microplastics and Health Effects: A Review of the Literature”

Summary: This review analyzed current research on the helth effects of exposure to microplastics and highlighted potential risks such as inflammation, oxidative stress and the absorption of toxic chemicals associated with microplastics.

Study Title “Perfluoroalkyl Substances and Cardiovascular Disease: A Review of the Epidemiological Evidence”

Summary: This review examined epidemiological studies investigating the association between exposure to perfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) and cardiovascular diseases, suggesting a possible link between PFAS exposure and increased risk of heart disease and related conditions.
