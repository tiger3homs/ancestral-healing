01

Before you build

The finding that reshapes this project
⚠ Policy collision

"Fully self-running" is the exact pattern YouTube is enforcing against.

YouTube's inauthentic content policy makes mass-produced, templated content ineligible for monetization — and enforcement now evaluates channels as a whole. The stated risk signals are upload frequency, format similarity, lack of commentary, and minimal editing.

Read that list against a fully automated pipeline. It maximises all four by default. Thousands of AI channels have already lost monetization this way; the penalty sequence runs warning → 90-day suspension → permanent removal.

It is not an AI ban. YouTube is explicitly tool-agnostic — AI-assisted content with real editorial decisions stays eligible. The line is human judgment, not tooling.

And your own data agrees. Your top three videos are lived personal experience and honest admission of difficulty — the one category AI cannot generate. The sharpest evidence:

"9 Ways To Connect With Your Ancestors Through Food"
1,100
"9 WAYS TO ADD MORE FAT ON AN ANIMAL BASED DIET"
15,000

Same structure. 13× apart. The variable is lived specificity — which is precisely what a pipeline can't manufacture.

The catch on YouTube

There is no one-click YouTube connector in Claude's directory. It takes a custom MCP server and a paid plan. Start with Windsor for analytics (free, 60-second setup), add Composio when you need write access. Watch the Data API quota — 10,000 units/day, and search calls cost 100 units each against 1 for a channel listing.

Plus four custom skills you write yourself — these are not connectors, and they're where the actual value lives: ah-script-writer, ah-thumbnail, ah-metadata, and ah-factcheck. That last one is the difference between a pipeline that scales your voice and one that scales your liability.

Policy risk at this volume

Break-even is roughly 57,000 views/month at a $5 RPM against Standard costs. At your current median that's ~42,000 from 8 long-form — so ad revenue alone doesn't cover it.

Which is the argument for quality over volume: one video at 71,000 views beats thirteen at 5,300, and costs less to make. The archive already holds three lead magnets and an ebook — a $27 guide converting at 1% of 100,000 views is $27,000, which dominates anything RPM does.

Billing trap worth knowing

If an ANTHROPIC_API_KEY is set in your environment, Claude Code silently bills the API instead of your subscription — you pay twice while the plan sits unused. Check your shell profile before subscribing.

Stop conditions

Roll back if: scripts stop sounding like you · you're rubber-stamping the gate rather than reading · costs exceed 2× projection twice running · any YouTube policy warning · performance drops below your historical median for 3+ consecutive videos.

That last one matters most. If AI-assisted videos underperform your manual ones, the pipeline subtracts value no matter how much time it saves.
